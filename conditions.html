Chronic Kidney Disease

Protecting Kidney Function Through Early Intervention

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a progressive condition where kidney function gradually declines over time.

Early diagnosis, blood pressure control, diabetes management and regular monitoring can significantly slow progression and help avoid complications.

Common Signs

  • ✓ Swelling in legs and feet
  • ✓ Fatigue and weakness
  • ✓ High blood pressure
  • ✓ Changes in urination
  • ✓ Loss of appetite

Why BP Control Matters

High blood pressure is one of the leading causes of kidney damage and can also worsen existing kidney disease.

Acute Kidney Injury

Timely Treatment Can Improve Recovery

Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) is a sudden decline in kidney function that requires prompt evaluation and treatment.

Long-term follow-up is important because some patients remain at risk for future kidney problems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Patient Questions

Can kidney disease be prevented?

In many cases, early screening, blood pressure control, diabetes management and healthy lifestyle habits can reduce risk and slow progression.

Does high blood pressure affect kidneys?

Yes. Uncontrolled hypertension can damage kidney blood vessels and reduce kidney function over time.

When is dialysis needed?

Dialysis may be recommended when kidney function becomes severely reduced and cannot adequately remove waste and excess fluid.
