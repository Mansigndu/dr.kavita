Frequently Asked Questions

Answers To Common Kidney Health Questions

Get quick answers to some of the most commonly asked questions regarding kidney disease, blood pressure, dialysis, and nephrology consultations.

When should I consult a nephrologist?

You should consult a nephrologist if you have chronic kidney disease, diabetes, high blood pressure, abnormal kidney function tests, protein in urine, kidney stones, swelling in the body, or recurrent urinary tract infections.

Can kidney disease be prevented?

Many kidney diseases can be prevented or slowed through early diagnosis, blood pressure control, diabetes management, adequate hydration, healthy nutrition, and regular health check-ups.

Does high blood pressure affect kidneys?

Yes. Uncontrolled hypertension can damage the blood vessels inside the kidneys over time, reducing their ability to filter waste effectively and increasing the risk of chronic kidney disease.

How often should kidney function be checked?

Individuals with diabetes, hypertension, family history of kidney disease, or previous kidney issues should undergo periodic kidney health evaluations as advised by their nephrologist.
