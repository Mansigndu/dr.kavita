Importance of Patient Education in Kidney Care

Patient education plays a crucial role in the prevention and management of kidney diseases. Well-informed patients are better equipped to understand their condition and participate actively in their treatment plan.

These educational resources are curated and reviewed by Dr. Kavita Vishwakarma, DM (Nephrology) and are based on current, evidence-based medical guidelines.

This content is intended for educational purposes only and should not replace consultation with your treating nephrologist.

Quick Patient Guide

CKD Do’s & Don’ts

Do’s

  • ✔ Take medicines regularly as prescribed
  • ✔ Monitor blood pressure routinely
  • ✔ Maintain light physical activity
  • ✔ Follow a balanced kidney-friendly diet
  • ✔ Stay hydrated as advised by your doctor
  • ✔ Do regular blood and urine tests
  • ✔ Consult a nephrologist or dietitian regularly

Don’ts

  • ✖ Avoid self-medication
  • ✖ Do not take painkillers without medical advice
  • ✖ Avoid unverified internet diets
  • ✖ Do not stop medicines on your own
  • ✖ Ignore swelling or breathlessness
  • ✖ Avoid unsupervised herbal medicines
  • ✖ Do not miss follow-up visits

Medical Disclaimer

The information provided in these resources is intended for educational and awareness purposes only. Kidney disease varies from patient to patient, and individual treatment may differ based on clinical evaluation.

This content should not be considered a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Patients are advised to consult their nephrologist or treating physician before making any changes to medications, diet, or lifestyle.
